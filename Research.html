Will Wright

Graduate Student

Department of Mathematics

I am currently studying Rayleigh quotient-type (RQ-type) optimization problems, which are dual to parameterized eigenvalue optimization problems. This equivalence gives a new interpretation of parameterized eigenvalue problems as RQ-type problems. One open topic is determining the necessary and sufficient conditions for solvability of RQ-type problems (i.e., boundedness of the infimum and attainment of a finite eigenvector).

Additionally, a couple different methods have been used to prove strong duality holds for a subset of these primal-dual pairs. One of these methods [2] uses SDP duality to prove strong duality holds for the trust region subproblem. Another more recent proof method [1] relies fundamentally on the S-procedure. Yet one of the several proofs of the S-lemma [3] relies on the strong duality of the linear SDP primal-dual pair. Then one might ask what other ways duality may be shown between RQ-type problems and parameterized eigenvalue problems. And more generally, can this duality be folded into conic duality theory?
